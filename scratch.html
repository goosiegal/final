Why I love to Silversmith: Creating something tangible from an idea reminds me of God. I feel a closeness to him as I am forging pieces of silver in to something much more beautiful. This resonates deeply with me as I feel like I am a frail piece of scrap metal waiting to be molded into a magnificant creation by the most perfect creator.

Steps
  1. Getting Started
    • Choose the stone you would like to use
    • Measure out the amount of bezel metal you will need to fit around the stone
    • Place a piece of solder on the inside of the shaped bezel
    • Turn the torch on and heat the entire bezel by moving the torch in circular motions
    • Heat the bezel until you see the solder melt and "zip" up the place where the two edges of the bezel meet
    • Put the bezel in water and then onto the table
    • Take the bezel and measure a piece of scrap metal to fit as the base plate where the stone will rest
    • Hammer the baseplate until it is flat
    • Place the bezel on the baseplate and add pieces of solder to the inside edges of the bezel
    • Solder the bezel to the base plate
    • Once you see the solder "zip" around the edges of the baseplate, remove the heat and put the entire soldered piece in water
    • Using scissors, cute the excess base plate off as close to the bezel as possible
    • Sand off the leftover baseplate
    • Using a ring sizer, measure the size of the band and cut
    • Hammer the band into a circle using the ring mandrel
    • Using a file or sanding machine smooth the two end the band
    • Turn the ring upside down with the bezel touching the table
    • Place the band on the back side of the ring and place two pieces of solder on either side of the inside contact points
    • Solder the band into place and promptly dunk the ring in water
    • Take a dot of super glue and place it inside the bezel cup
    • Place the stone inside and press firmly on the stone and baseplate for 5 minutes
    • Once the glue is dry, use the bezel pusher to wrap the bezel around the stone
    • Polish the ring and you are good to go
